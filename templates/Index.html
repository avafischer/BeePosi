Effects of Screens on the Brain


Screens are insidiously impairing users’ ability to interact with the world around them, enjoy the beautiful things that nature has to offer and creating shortsightedness that produces a clouded way of interpreting life. A significant factor that impairs this generation’s functionality is addictive behaviour when it comes to screen use. When users are on their screens, a specific neurotransmitter called Dopamine is released: It keeps users going back repeatedly to the source of their addiction. If we work to mitigate dopamine levels that are released when people are attached to their devices, society can regain balance within their brain chemistry.
